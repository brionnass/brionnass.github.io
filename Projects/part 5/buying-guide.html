Sunscreen Buying Guide

Mineral vs. Chemical Sunscreens

Category Mineral Sunscreen Chemical Sunscreen
Active Ingredients Zinc Oxide, Titanium Dioxide Oxybenzone, Avobenzone, Octinoxate, Homosalate
How it Works Physically blocks and reflects UV rays Absorbs UV rays and converts them into heat
Best For Sensitive skin, children, and those looking for natural ingredients Daily wear, sports, and those who prefer a lightweight feel
Time to Effectiveness Effective immediately upon application Requires 20 minutes to fully absorb
Texture May leave a white cast or thicker texture Lightweight, often invisible on the skin
Environmental Impact Reef-safe, does not harm coral reefs May contain ingredients harmful to marine life

Choosing the Right SPF Level

SPF (Sun Protection Factor) measures how well a sunscreen will protect your skin from UVB rays. Hereâ€™s a quick guide to choosing the right SPF:

  • SPF 15-30: Suitable for daily use, offering moderate protection against sunburn.
  • SPF 30-50: Provides higher protection, ideal for extended outdoor activities.
  • SPF 50+: Maximum protection, recommended for sensitive skin or intense sun exposure.

Application Tips for Maximum Protection

To get the most out of your sunscreen, follow these application tips:

  • Apply sunscreen 15-20 minutes before sun exposure to allow it to absorb into the skin.
  • Reapply every 2 hours, or immediately after swimming or sweating.
  • Use a generous amount of sunscreen to cover all exposed areas, including often-missed spots like the ears, neck, and the tops of your feet.
