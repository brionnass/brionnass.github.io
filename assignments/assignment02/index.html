Why You Should Wear Sunscreen

Sunscreen is an essential part of your daily skincare routine.Sunscreen is a crucial part of any skincare routine because it provides a protective barrier against the sun's harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays. These rays are classified into two types: UVA and UVB. UVA rays can prematurely age your skin, causing wrinkles and age spots, while UVB rays are responsible for sunburn. Both types contribute to the risk of developing skin cancer. By applying sunscreen with broad-spectrum protection, you can shield your skin from these damaging effects. Regular use of sunscreen not only helps prevent sunburn but also reduces the risk of long-term skin damage and the development of skin cance

By using sunscreen regularly, you reduce your risk of developing skin issues and maintain healthier, younger-looking skin. It's important to choose a sunscreen with broad-spectrum protection and a suitable SPF for your skin type.When selecting a sunscreen, it's important to choose one with an appropriate Sun Protection Factor (SPF) for your skin type and exposure level. SPF measures the level of protection the sunscreen provides against UVB rays. Dermatologists generally recommend using a sunscreen with at least SPF 30, which blocks about 97% of UVB rays. Additionally, it's important to apply sunscreen generously and reapply every two hours, especially after swimming or sweating.
